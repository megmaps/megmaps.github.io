About Me.

I am a Master's student at the Univeristy of British Columbia specializing in Geological Engineering. That said... I consider myself more of an aspiring geoscientist who loves to make maps. I completed an undergraduate degree in Geographical Science where I focused on topics in GIS, remote sensnig, and fluvial geomorphology. Now, my studies focus on classifying Debris Floods in BC through field work and remote sensing applications.

Here, you can find some of her past academic work, professional acomplishements, and some personal anecdotes. Thanks for reading:)

Tutorials

2024 Feb

Undertsnading Drainage Systems

This workshop explored the Hydrological Toolkit in ArcGIS. Participants learned how to use various tools including Flow Accumulation, Flow Direction, Stream Order, and Watershed Delineation. And it all starts with a DEM!
Designed and taught by Megan Elkin
2023 Nov

Exploring LiDAR Data

This workshop dove into the world of point clouds and LiDAR datasets. Participants learned how/where to download LiDAR data in British Columbia, and convert their datasets into a format compatible with ArcGIS software. Participants learned how to create DSM and DTM bare earth surfaces and complete a simple tree canopy height analysis.
Designed and taught by Megan Elkin
2024 March

Distorted Worlds

Ever wanted to just play with maps? Well then throw away all those scaling rules and design principles and come have fun! This workshop allowed participants to create thier own "Distorted World" starting with a bare earth surface. Channel your Middle Earth adventurer, and come make some fun maps!
Designed and taught by Megan Elkin, Dillon Johnson and Ciara Norton
